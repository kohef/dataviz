What is Data Visualization and why do it

In short, data viz is a representation of data in graphical format. It helps in simplifying complex data so as to allow for exploratory analysis where new patterns and insights might be uncovered. This reader offers a concise and clear introduction to the world of data visualization.

Data Viz Essentials

What is your compelling story

Consider what question are you asking about the data. Is it an exploratory chart where you are trying to make sense of the data set or testing out an hypothesis? Or is it an explanatory chart where you are sharing your insights with the audience

What type of data are you working with

Continuous or categorical, text or numbers, spatial or temporal, network?

How many variables do you want to visualise at once

Limited attention span to process more than three to four variables at one time

What type of pattern are you trying to show

Comparison, trends or correlation, proportion or composition, hierarchy or change over time

Data Viz examples

Using data from world bank, we visualize the rise of health expenditure in Singapore, across sectors over the years. The first area graph chart is able to show an overall increased rate of health expenditure across all sectors but the line graph is able to explicitly show the increased rate of government expenditure in Singapore since 2012.

Visualizing changes across time

This time round we utilise Flourish studio apps to create a Hans Rosling inspired chart, whereby we visualize the changes across countries on health related expenditures. We ask the question of how age depdendency ratio is related to health expenditures, both government and individual's out of pocket expenditure basis. Five variables are represented on this scatter visualization, age-dependency ratio, government health expenditure, countries by colour, years through time interactivity, out of pocket expenditure by size of scatter dot.

The visualization shows that the relationship between age dependency ratio and health expenditure varies across countries and would be an indication of effectiveness of healthcare policies in each country:

  • Switzerland, New Zealand, Germany and United States have similar age dependency ratios, yet the government expenditures ranges from less than US$2,000/capita to around US$5,000/capita.
  • Switzerland has one of the lowest government health expenditure per capita but the individual's out of pocket health expenditure is the highest. US has the highest government health expenditure, yet the individual's out of pocket is the 2nd highest after Switzerland.
  • Meanwhile, Singapore had the lowest age dependency ratio due to the reliance on foreign workforce, thus masking the actual high age dependency ratio that its citizens face. Though the government's health expenditure is lowest among the six countries, the individual's out of pocket health expenditure is 3rd highest, after Switzerland and US.
